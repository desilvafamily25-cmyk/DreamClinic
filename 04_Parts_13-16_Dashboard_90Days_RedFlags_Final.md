## PART 13 — OWNER DASHBOARD

### Overview

The Owner Dashboard is your single control point. This section defines what you must review, when, and why. All items should be in a shared dashboard tool (Excel, Notion, Monday.com, or Xero) that auto-updates where possible.

**Key Principle:** You cannot manage what you do not measure. The dashboard is your early-warning system.

---

### 13.1 DAILY DASHBOARD (Review by 9:00 AM Every Workday)

| Metric | Target/Alert | How to Check | Red Flag | Action if Red Flag |
|--------|--------------|--------------|----------|-------------------|

| **Consultation Count by GP** | 15–22 consults per GP per day (depending on session length) | Practice management system (PMC) report | Any GP <12 or >28 | Check for overbooking, cancellations, or system issues |

### 13.2 WEEKLY DASHBOARD (Review Every Friday or Monday Morning)

| Metric | Target | How to Check | Red Flag | Owner Action |
|--------|--------|--------------|----------|--------------|
| **Weekly Billings Total** | $40,000–60,000 (depending on FTE, billing model, and patient mix) | Xero P&L or weekly PMC report | <$32,000 or >$70,000 | Investigate billing codes; check for duplicates; review GP utilisation |
| **Weekly Billings vs YTD Target** | Track cumulative variance | Xero rolling 12-week forecast | 10%+ below target for 2+ weeks | Schedule urgent review with accountant; adjust GP hours or billing |
| **Banking Reconciliation** | 100% match between bank and PMC system | Xero bank reconciliation tool | Any unreconciled item >$5,000 | Contact bookkeeper; review duplicate claims or reversals |
| **Doctor Sessions Completed vs Rostered** | 95%+ completion (allow <5% for emergencies/sickness) | Compare roster to PMC | <90% completion | Review GP satisfaction; check for scheduling issues |
| **Payroll Exceptions & Timesheet Approvals** | 100% timesheets approved; no unpaid wages outstanding | Payroll software (Xero, Quicken, etc.) | Any unsigned timesheet or late payment | Approve immediately; investigate delays |
| **Open Complaints Register** | 0 overdue complaints; all <7 days | Complaints log (Excel or system) | Any complaint >14 days open | Escalate; brief complainant on progress |
| **Staffing Gaps Next Week** | None (or planned/covered) | Upcoming roster + leave calendar | Any unexpected gap or no-show pattern | Book relief/casual; discuss with affected GP |
| **Stock Levels – Vaccines** | All cold-chain items in stock; no expiry alerts | Vaccine fridge log + inventory system | Any expired or near-expiry | Order immediately; review VCPR; document cold chain incident |
| **Stock Levels – Consumables** | Critical items (gloves, swabs, syringes) in stock | Physical check or system | Any item out of stock | Emergency order; review supplier SLA |
| **Appointment Utilisation Rate** | 85%+ of slots booked (6+ weeks forward) | PMC booking report | <75% utilisation | Review capacity; promote services; adjust pricing if needed |
| **Overdue Accounts Payable** | 0 (or <$500 emergency hold) | Xero AP aging report | Any invoice >30 days overdue | Pay immediately; review supplier terms; assess if negotiation needed |
| **Unresolved Patient Safety Reports** | 0 overdue | Incident/safety register | Any >7 days open | Escalate to clinical lead; brief team; file with accreditation body if required |

**Weekly Dashboard Owner Review Checklist:**

- [ ] Review billings trend (is it stable? growing? declining?)
- [ ] Check banking reconciliation (are there unexplained gaps?)
- [ ] Verify all staff were paid on time
- [ ] Confirm no open complaints or incidents
- [ ] Check vaccine/consumable stock (especially if >1 branch)
- [ ] Verify GP session completion rate (morale check)
- [ ] Review appointment utilisation
- [ ] Brief practice manager on any gaps
- [ ] Update forecast if variance detected

**Owner Time Investment:** 30–45 minutes.

## PART 14 — FIRST 90 DAYS ACTION PLAN AFTER ACQUISITION

### Overview

The first 90 days post-acquisition are critical. Your goal is **stabilisation, not transformation**. Do not attempt major operational changes in month 1; focus on securing access, understanding the business, and identifying gaps. Months 2–3 involve deeper analysis and preparation for longer-term optimisation.

**Guiding Principle:** Trust but verify. The seller may have misrepresented aspects of the business. Your job is to confirm everything is as represented—and to identify what was not disclosed.

---

### 14.1 DAYS 1–7: CRITICAL STABILISATION

| Task | Why It Matters | How to Do It | What to Look For | Red Flag | Success Indicator |
|------|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Secure All Access Credentials** | Cannot run clinic without access to banking, software, Medicare, email, domain | Request from seller agent: banking logins, PMC admin password, email domain access, Medicare portal credentials, HPOS/PRODA access, alarm codes, building keys, safe combination | Test each login immediately; verify you have admin rights; confirm 2FA is set up | Any account with seller still holding access; missing MFA | You can independently reset passwords; multi-user access confirmed |
| **Meet Every Staff Member Individually (1:1 meetings, 15 min each)** | Build rapport; understand any concerns; identify flight risks or cultural issues; learn informal processes | Schedule before clinic starts or at lunch; ask: "What's working well?", "What's frustrating?", "Any concerns about the transition?", "Do you plan to stay?" | Body language; eye contact; hesitation on future plans; mention of undisclosed arrangements (cash payments, informal hours) | Any staff member expressing plans to leave; undisclosed cash arrangements; confusion about pay/hours | All staff express confidence; no surprise departures in month 1 |
| **Confirm All GP Contractor Agreements Are Signed & Current** | Ensures you are the legal employer; understand payment obligations; identify verbal arrangements that could create liability | Collect signed contracts from practice records; review each for: term, termination clause, restraint clause, non-compete, IP ownership, payment % or FTE rate, notice period, dispute resolution | Look for: expiry dates, unsigned agreements, inconsistency between contract and actual billings, missing restraint clauses, unclear good faith payment obligations | Any unsigned agreement; verbal arrangement undisclosed; contract term expiring within 12 months without renewal language | All GPs have signed, current agreements; no surprises in payment obligations |
| **Verify All Insurance Certificates Are Valid** | Protect clinic from liability; comply with lease and professional requirements | Request from seller: medical indemnity certificate (verify practitioner names match), public liability certificate, professional indemnity (if applicable), property insurance, cyber liability (recommended) | Check: expiry dates, coverage amounts ($5M+ typical for medical indemnity), named insured matches your entity | Any certificate expiring within 30 days; low coverage limits (<$2M); GP names missing | All policies current; coverage adequate; certificates stored electronically |
| **Confirm Lease Is Properly Assigned/Transferred** | Ensures you have legal occupancy; avoids eviction risk; clarifies outgoings obligations | Request: executed lease assignment document from seller's lawyer; landlord consent letter; copy of current lease with all amendments; latest outgoings reconciliation | Check: your name/entity as new tenant; landlord signature; any break clauses or renewal options; rent review clauses (CPI?); outgoings cap | Lease not yet assigned to you; landlord not formally notified; ambiguous outgoings language | Lease formally assigned; landlord acknowledgment email received |
| **Review Bank Accounts & Signatories** | Ensure you control clinic finances; prevent fraud; understand cash position | Visit bank in person; request: list of all accounts (operating, savings, loan), current signatories, recent 6-month statement, any loan/facility agreements | Check: your name added as signatory; seller removed or in second position; account names match clinic entity; no unexplained large transfers | Seller still sole signatory; joint signatory without your consent; unexplained low balance (<1 week payroll) | You are primary signatory; statements auto-download to Xero; no other signatories without approval |
| **Verify Payroll Is Set Up Correctly Under New Entity** | Ensures employees are paid on-time; minimizes ATO issues; confirms no arrears or underpayments | Meet with payroll provider or bookkeeper; request: proof payroll is processing under your ABN/entity name, latest payslip examples, superannuation arrangement (fund name, account details), PAYG withholding settings | Check: employee names and amounts match staff records, super contributions ≥11.5%, tax file numbers on file, YTD figures reconcile to timesheets | Payroll still running under seller's entity; super not being paid; missing TFNs | Payroll processes cleanly; staff all paid by day 5 of month; no ATO correspondence |
| **Check Accreditation Status & Next Cycle Date** | Know your compliance baseline; understand what you inherit; plan for next audit | Contact RACGP or accreditation body; request: copy of current accreditation certificate, last survey report, any non-conformances/corrective actions, planned next survey date | Read survey report carefully: what areas were flagged? Are corrective actions completed? When is next survey? What's the deadline to be re-accredited? | Practice is not accredited; survey is overdue; critical non-conformances not addressed | Accreditation current; corrective actions clearly tracked; next survey planned 6+ months ahead |
| **Secure Physical Keys, Alarm Codes, Safe Combinations** | Cannot access clinic for operations; security liability; staff cannot enter/exit | Request from seller/landlord: set of clinic keys (front, back, server room, drug safe, filing cabinets), alarm system code, alarm system login/admin details, safe combination (if applicable), CCTV/security login | Test each key; disarm/re-arm alarm; change all codes/combinations immediately | Seller retains keys; alarm code unchanged; safe lock broken | New key set created; alarm reprogrammed; safe tested; old codes destroyed |
| **Review Upcoming Roster for Next 4 Weeks** | Identify scheduling issues, gaps, or overcommitment; prevent patient access problems | Request from practice manager: roster for next 28 days, any known leave or part-time patterns, any GPs on notice or planning change in hours | Look for: gaps (especially if multi-branch), clustering of GP absences, rostering inconsistencies with signed contracts, any sessions not yet confirmed | Any week with <FTE GPs rostered; no contingency for casual coverage; unconfirmed sessions | Roster complete and confirmed; all GPs have signed off; relief staff pre-arranged for known absences |
| **Identify Any Immediate Compliance Gaps** | Know what's broken before it becomes a bigger issue; avoid surprise audits or patient harm | Conduct quick review: AHPRA registration of all GPs (online check), insurance certificates validity, cold chain compliance (fridge temps logged?), hand hygiene audit, incident register (any overdue follow-ups?), privacy policy currency (updated for Australian Privacy Principles?) | Document each finding in a checklist; flag as critical, high, or low risk | Any GP not registered; insurance lapsed; fridge temperature logs missing; incident register not current | Compliance checklist completed; all critical items assigned an owner and deadline |
| **Set Up Your Own PRODA/HPOS Access** | Direct access to Medicare rebate data; ability to review claiming independently; dispute resolution | Register for PRODA (Provider Digital Access) or HPOS (Health Professional Online Services); request access to clinic's Medicare account | Verify you can log in; view recent claiming; access provider number details; set up delegate permissions | You cannot access clinic's Medicare data; access denied for provider number | You can independently view clinic's claiming history and balance owing |
| **Confirm Pathology Agreement Is Transferred** | Ensures clinic receives rebate income; know collection/referral arrangements; avoid service interruptions | Request: signed pathology agreement (e.g., Healthscope, Sonic, local pathology), confirmation of transfer to new entity, commission/rebate rates, collection schedule | Check: your entity named as provider; agreement term and renewal dates; any performance minimums; weekly/monthly settlement timing | Pathology agreement still in seller's name; unclear commission structure; no written agreement | Agreement transferred; commission reconciliation understood; collection samples flowing smoothly |
| **DO NOT MAKE OPERATIONAL CHANGES YET** | Premature changes cause staff uncertainty, patient disruption, and unintended consequences; need baseline data to decide what to change | Make a commitment: no staffing changes, pricing changes, service changes, or policy updates until day 31+ | Monitor for the urge to "fix" things; document any issues for later review | Staff leaving due to uncertainty; patients complaining about unexpected changes | You have baseline metrics; staff feel stable; patient feedback is neutral |

### 16.4 CRITICAL QUESTIONS TO ASK SELLER (In Writing)

Beyond the document list above, ask the seller to answer these questions in writing (you may want to use these as a statutory declaration). Get answers before settlement if possible; if after settlement, within 7 days.

| # | Question | Why It Matters | How to Use Answer |
|---|----------|----------------|-------------------|
| 1 | Are all GPs currently working under signed contractor agreements? Any verbal arrangements or letter agreements only? | Identifies unsigned arrangements that could create disputes | If any unsigned: insist on signed agreement before paying that GP |
| 2 | Have any GPs given notice to leave or expressed intent to leave in the past 12 months? | Identifies flight risk; allows you to plan recruitment or re-negotiate | If flight risk identified: engage GP early; understand concerns; offer retention if key |
| 3 | Are there any undisclosed financial arrangements with GPs (e.g., loans, advances, personal guarantees for equipment)? | Identifies hidden liabilities you may inherit | If any: document in settlement; negotiate indemnity if liability-bearing |
| 4 | Are all staff correctly classified under relevant awards (nursing award, health services award)? Have pay rates been verified as compliant? | Identifies backpay or compliance risk | Request evidence of award compliance review; obtain indemnity if risk |
| 5 | Have any staff been paid below NMW or without correct super contributions? Any backpay owing? | Identifies wage theft or non-compliance liability | If any: document; obtain indemnity from seller; rectify immediately |
| 6 | Are there any outstanding workers' compensation claims or WHS incidents that haven't been resolved? | Identifies liability or pending claims | Request details; obtain indemnity; notify your insurer |
| 7 | Has the clinic received any compliance letters from Medicare, AHPRA, RACGP, health complaints body, or VRMA? | Identifies regulatory issues you're inheriting | If any: obtain full correspondence; understand resolution; obtain indemnity if unresolved |
| 8 | Are there any disputes, complaints, or legal claims against the clinic or GPs (patient litigation, FWA claims, landlord disputes, supplier disputes)? | Identifies pending litigation or disputes | Request details; obtain indemnity; brief your lawyer |
| 9 | Does the lease have any unusual clauses, break options, or renewal restrictions the buyer should know about? | Identifies lease risks | Clarify all key terms; negotiate extension if break/renewal imminent |
| 10 | Has the landlord agreed to the assignment of the lease to the new owner, or is assignment still pending? | Confirms you'll have legal occupancy post-settlement | Confirm in writing from landlord before settlement |
| 11 | Are there any planned building renovations, maintenance, or landlord disputes that could disrupt clinic operations? | Identifies operational risks | Plan for contingencies; maintain normal operations if renovation underway |
| 12 | Has the pathology relationship been confirmed in writing as transferring to the new owner? Are commission rates and rebate arrangements clear? | Confirms pathology supply and revenue flow | Confirm transfer in writing from pathology provider; verify agreement transferred |
| 13 | Are there any undisclosed debts, loans, finance agreements, or personal guarantees the clinic is liable for? | Identifies financial liability you may inherit | Request full disclosure; obtain indemnity; arrange refinancing if needed |
| 14 | Has the clinic ever been subject to an ATO audit, payroll tax audit, or other regulatory investigation? | Identifies audit risk or compliance issues | Request details; obtain indemnity; brief your accountant |
| 15 | Are there any patient safety incidents or near-misses in the last 12 months that haven't been formally reported or investigated? | Identifies unreported incidents or systemic issues | Request incident summary; investigate if concerning; report to accreditor if required |
| 16 | Are there any key staff members who might leave soon, or any retention concerns you should know about? | Identifies flight risk | Engage key staff early; understand concerns; offer retention if needed |
| 17 | What is the typical patient profile (demographics, growth rate, retention rate)? Any loss of major patient groups in the past 12 months? | Identifies patient base stability | Analyse patient data; plan for growth or retention if needed |
| 18 | Are there any supplier relationships or arrangements that could be affected by the change of ownership? Any commitments you haven't disclosed? | Identifies supplier risks | Confirm all supplier relationships transfer smoothly; renegotiate if terms unfavourable |
| 19 | Are there any undisclosed verbal arrangements with staff, GPs, or suppliers that you consider important? | Identifies informal arrangements you need to formalise | Formalise all arrangements in writing post-settlement |
| 20 | If I find undisclosed items or misrepresentations post-settlement, how will disputes be resolved? | Clarifies dispute resolution process | Ensure indemnity language is clear; agree on dispute resolution mechanism in purchase agreement |

### Summary

This 90-day action plan and dashboard framework provides a comprehensive roadmap for a new GP clinic owner to:

1. **Secure the business** (days 1–7): Gain access and control
2. **Understand the business** (days 8–30): Deep analysis and documentation
3. **Optimise operations** (days 31–60): Implement systems and controls

- **Trust but verify:** Seller representations should be validated against independent data
- **Document everything:** Legal clarity on all major arrangements
- **Delegate ruthlessly:** Practice manager, bookkeeper, and reception lead handle day-to-day
- **Measure relentlessly:** Dashboard discipline enables early intervention
- **Comply rigorously:** RACGP accreditation and regulatory requirements are non-negotiable
- **Communicate clearly:** Weekly staff meetings, monthly investor updates, transparent decision-making

- Secured all access and documentation
