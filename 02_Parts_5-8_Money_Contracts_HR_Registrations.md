# PART 5: MONEY FLOW MAP

## Overview
Every dollar that enters a GP practice comes from predictable sources and must flow through documented channels. This section maps WHO pays WHAT, WHEN, HOW, and WHAT reconciles to WHAT. Operator responsibility: weekly cash review against these flows.

---

## 5.1 PATIENT BILLING & MEDICARE CLAIMING

### Bulk-Billed Claims (No Gap to Patient)

**Standard Consultation Item Numbers:**
- **Item 23** – Standard GP consultation, < 10 mins
- **Item 36** – Standard GP consultation, 10–20 mins (most common)
- **Item 44** – Extended GP consultation, > 20 mins
- **Item 47** – Home visit (time-based variants exist)

**How money flows:**
1. Receptionist registers patient, records item number (based on GP time in room)
2. GP provides service
3. Claim submitted to Medicare (via Best Practice/MD system or direct batch)
4. Medicare pays practice directly (100% of MBS fee)
5. Patient pays nothing

**MBS rebate rates (April 2026):**  
Source: Expert knowledge / **External verification needed** (exact rates update quarterly)
- Item 23: ~AUD $45 (example)
- Item 36: ~AUD $60 (example)
- Item 44: ~AUD $90 (example)

**Cash timing:**
- Claims submitted daily or batched
- Medicare ECLIPSE payment cycle: 5–7 business days typically
- Bank receipt: 7–10 days after submission (processor delay)

**Operator control points:**
- **Weekly:** reconcile items billed vs items claimed (match count/value)
- **Monthly:** Medicare bank statement vs claims register (gap = claim failure or delay)
- **Red flags:**
  - Unbilled services (patient seen, no claim recorded)
  - Item code errors (e.g., Item 23 claiming when Item 36 was appropriate)
  - Rejected claims (submitted but denied by Medicare – causes 2–3 week investigation)

---

### Medicare Claims – ECLIPSE & PRODA

**System overview:**
- **ECLIPSE:** Medicare's claims processing system; practices submit via approved software (Best Practice, MD, Hotdoc, Pracsoft, etc.)
- **PRODA:** Practice Registration and Disclosures Authority – practice delegates user accounts; tracks who can submit claims

**Claim submission process:**
1. Practice staff log into PRODA portal, confirm delegations to practice manager + bookkeeper
2. Practice staff log into ECLIPSE via software (Best Practice, etc.)
3. Claims submitted (batch daily, or real-time in modern systems)
4. Medicare responds with acceptance/rejection within 24 hours (usually)
5. Accepted claims queued for payment

**Payment deposit:**
- Typically into business bank account designated for Medicare
- Often separate account to practice operational account (for reconciliation)

**Operator responsibility:**
- Confirm PRODA delegations annually (who has submit rights? who can receive payment details?)
- Reconcile ECLIPSE reports weekly (what was sent vs what was accepted)
- Investigate rejected claims immediately (often due to missing/incorrect provider number, item code mismatch, or patient non-eligibility)
- Ensure software integrates with bank feeds (so Medicare deposits auto-reconcile)

---

### Mixed Billing (Bulk-Bill + Private Gap)

**How it works:**
- Patient bulk-billed for consultation (e.g., Item 36 = AUD $60 Medicare rebate)
- GP also charges private gap fee (e.g., AUD $40 additional)
- Patient pays gap; practice receives Medicare + gap

**Cash timing:**
- Medicare: 5–10 days (as above)
- Gap fee: usually same-day (EFTPOS at reception) or invoiced (30 days)

**Operator control points:**
- **Daily:** EFTPOS reconciliation (gap fees collected vs EFTPOS receipts)
- **Weekly:** gap fee invoices sent and follow-up on unpaid
- **Monthly:** gap fee bank deposits vs billing register
- **Red flags:**
  - Gap fee charged but not recorded in billing system
  - Gap fees collected but not deposited
  - Patient claims "wasn't told about gap" (staff training issue)

---

### Private Billing (No Medicare Claim)

**When used:**
- Patient not eligible for Medicare (e.g., overseas visitor, incorrect Medicare number)
- Patient choice (may occur for cosmetic/non-core services)
- Some allied health

**Typical fees (Victorian practice benchmark):**  
Source: Expert knowledge / **External verification needed**
- Standard consultation: AUD $120–$180
- Extended: AUD $180–$250
- Home visit: AUD $200–$350 + travel

**Cash timing:**
- Usually EFTPOS same-day
- May be invoiced (30 days) – higher default risk

**Operator control points:**
- **At registration:** confirm patient Medicare status; if not eligible, inform of private fee
- **Daily:** EFTPOS reconciliation
- **Monthly:** invoice tracking and debt collection follow-up

---

### Chronic Disease Management (CDM) Item Numbers

**Items:**
- **Item 721:** Initial health assessment GP (>45 mins, plan created)
- **Item 723:** Follow-up CDM GP visit
- **Item 732:** Nurse-led review

**Value:** Higher than standard consultation; often AUD $100+ per item

**Eligibility:** Patient must have chronic condition, documented plan, usually referred by GP

**How they differ:**
- Requires detailed documentation (plan must be in record)
- Often bundled with specialist referral
- Can be bulk-billed or private (if claimed, must be correct item number)

**Operator control points:**
- **At claim time:** verify plan is documented (auditors will check)
- **Monthly:** CDM claims vs plans created (should be 1:1 or close match)
- **Red flag:** claiming CDM without documented plan = compliance risk

---

### Mental Health Items (Session-Based)

**Items:**
- **Item 2700:** Initial assessment (up to 60 mins, AUD ~$120+)
- **Item 2701:** Follow-up (up to 30 mins, AUD ~$90+)
- **Item 2702:** Group (AUD ~$65+)

**Key difference:** Often referred to allied health (psychologist, social worker), not just GP

**Operator control points:**
- **Referral tracking:** patient referred, session attended, claim lodged
- **Item coding:** practitioners must be qualified (e.g., psychologist, counsellor); GP-referred mental health items have specific requirements
- **Red flag:** mental health claim without documented referral or session record

---

### Health Assessment Items (71-Year-Olds, etc.)

**Items:**
- **Item 701–707:** GP health assessment (varies by age/context; AUD ~$100+)

**Key requirement:** Specific assessment form completed, Medicare eligible

**Operator control points:**
- **Recall:** practice should proactively contact eligible patients
- **Documentation:** assessment form filed in record before claiming
- **Timing:** typically annual opportunity per patient

## 5.2 PRIVATE FEES & THIRD-PARTY BILLING

### WorkCover (Victorian Workplace Injury)

**How it works:**
- Employee injured at work → referred to WorkCover-approved provider
- Provider claims directly to WorkCover insurer (or patient bills WorkCover)
- No gap to patient

**Typical item value:** AUD $100–$150 per consultation

**Cash timing:**
- WorkCover insurer pays practice directly (or patient reimbursed)
- Often slower than Medicare (2–3 weeks)

**Operator control points:**
- **At registration:** confirm WorkCover claim number and insurer details
- **Claim submission:** use correct WorkCover item codes (not Medicare)
- **Monthly reconciliation:** WorkCover claims lodged vs payments received

**Red flags:**
- Claiming Medicare AND WorkCover for same visit (double-dipping)
- Patient treated but claim not lodged (patient waits for reimbursement)

---

### DVA (Department of Veterans' Affairs)

**Eligibility:** War widow/er, veteran, or dependent on DVA gold/white card

**How it works:**
- Similar to Medicare bulk-billing
- DVA pays practice directly
- Some DVA patients bulk-billed; others private with gap

**Operator control points:**
- **At registration:** confirm DVA card type (affects billing)
- **Claim submission:** use DVA provider number + DVA batch process (separate from Medicare)
- **Monthly:** DVA payments vs claims

---

### TAC (Transport Accident Commission – Victoria)

**Eligibility:** Person injured in motor vehicle accident covered by TAC

**How it works:**
- TAC claim number issued
- Provider claims TAC directly
- TAC pays practice

**Operator control points:**
- **Registration:** confirm TAC claim number
- **Item coding:** use TAC-appropriate item numbers
- **Monthly reconciliation:** claims vs payments

---

### Third-Party Private Insurance

**Common:** Overseas visitor insurance, accident insurance, etc.

**How it works:**
- Patient pays upfront; claims reimbursement from insurer
- OR practice claims insurer directly (requires pre-approval)

**Operator control points:**
- **Policy check:** confirm insurance active and covers GP services
- **Payment terms:** typically 30 days after submission
- **Red flags:** patient uninsured or insurance lapsed

## 5.3 MERCHANT SETTLEMENT & EFTPOS

### Daily EFTPOS Reconciliation

**Process:**
1. Each EFTPOS terminal (Tyro, Square, Commonwealth Bank, etc.) processes transactions throughout day
2. End-of-day batch settled
3. Funds deposited to nominated bank account (typically 1–2 business days)

**Operator control points:**
- **Daily:** print EFTPOS settlement reports; reconcile to cash register/POS system
- **Discrepancies:** gaps between POS and EFTPOS (indicate cash register errors, stolen transactions, or system failures)
- **Weekly:** EFTPOS bank deposit vs daily settlements (chase delays)

**Typical merchant fee:** 1.5–2.5% of transaction value (varies by provider)

**Reconciliation tools:**
- Bank feeds (software integrations with Best Practice, etc.) auto-import EFTPOS deposits
- Manual monthly reconciliation still recommended

## 5.4 CONTRACTOR GP PERCENTAGE PAYMENTS

### Typical Arrangement

**Structure:**
- GP is contractor (not employee)
- GP receives percentage of billings or fixed service fee
- Practice retains remainder

**Common split:**
- GP receives: 65–70% of billings
- Practice retains: 30–35% (covers rent, staff, utilities, admin)

**Example:**
- Item 36 bulk-billed: AUD $60 Medicare receipt
- GP receives: AUD $42 (70%)
- Practice retains: AUD $18 (30%)

**Payment frequency:** Weekly, fortnightly, or monthly (varies by agreement)

**Operator control points:**
- **At hire:** confirm percentage split in signed contract
- **Weekly:** run GP billings report (Best Practice > Reports > GP Billings)
- **Reconcile:** items claimed this week vs GP payment due
- **Monthly:** GP payment invoice vs total billings
- **Red flags:**
  - GP payment doesn't match billings × percentage (indicates missing claims or data-entry error)
  - GP claims underpaid (check contract and confirm no disputed claims)

### Service Fee Alternative

**Structure:**
- GP paid flat fee (e.g., AUD $2,500/week) + percentage of *excess* billings
- Practice retains first $X in billings, then splits excess 50/50 (example)

**Reconciliation:** same as above but with fixed fee component factored in

## 5.5 EMPLOYEE GP WAGES

### Employment vs Contracting

**Employment (salaried GP):**
- PAYG tax withheld
- Superannuation paid (11.5% from 1 July 2025)
- Workers compensation insurance required
- Entitled to leave

**Contracting:**
- No tax withheld (GP pays own tax)
- No superannuation obligation
- No workers comp (contractor covers own insurance)
- No leave entitlements

**Operator responsibility:**
- Confirm structure with accountant
- Ensure correct tax treatment (ATO scrutiny on sham contracting in GP sector – see *Prince Developments* case law)

## 5.6 NURSE WAGES

### Award Coverage

**Applicable award:** Nurses Award 2020 (Fair Work Ombudsman)

**Classification:**
- RN (Registered Nurse, Level 1-6)
- EN (Enrolled Nurse, Level 1-4)
- Immunisation Nurse (often RN but specialist rate may apply)

**Minimum rates (April 2026):**  
Source: Expert knowledge / **External verification needed – check Fair Work Ombudsman**
- RN Level 1: ~AUD $65,000–$70,000 pa (example)
- EN Level 1: ~AUD $55,000–$60,000 pa (example)
- Penalty rates apply for evenings, weekends, public holidays

**Operator control points:**
- **At hire:** confirm AHPRA registration (RN/EN number)
- **Payroll check:** wage rate matches Award (run quarterly benchmarking)
- **Timesheets:** record hours worked; ensure no unpaid overtime
- **Superannuation:** 11.5% mandatory contribution
- **Red flags:**
  - RN paid below Award rate
  - AHPRA registration lapsed (nurse cannot legally practice)
  - Unrecorded casual shifts

## 5.9 SUPERANNUATION

### Employer Contribution Rate

**Current (1 July 2025 onwards):** 11.5%

**Applies to:**
- All employees (full-time, part-time, casual)
- Contractor GPs: NO contribution required

**Calculation:**
- Employee annual salary AUD $65,000 × 11.5% = AUD $7,475/year = ~AUD $620/month

**Operator control points:**
- **Monthly:** payroll system auto-calculates and deposits to employee SuperFunds
- **Reconciliation:** payroll summary should show total super paid vs total salary
- **Annual:** obtain ATO SuperFund payment receipts (reconcile to payroll records)
- **Red flags:**
  - Super not paid (breach of superannuation guarantee)
  - Underpayment (common error: wrong percentage or salary base)

## 5.10 PAYROLL TAX (VICTORIA)

### Threshold & Obligation

**Payroll tax applies if:**
- Victorian practice payroll > ~AUD $900,000/year (2026 threshold)

**Rate:** ~4.85% of payroll above threshold

**Operator control points:**
- **Quarterly:** calculate whether threshold exceeded
- **If threshold exceeded:** register with State Revenue Office Victoria; lodge quarterly returns
- **Red flags:**
  - Payroll crossing threshold but not registered (penalty interest accrues)
  - Contractor arrangements used to avoid payroll tax (ATO scrutiny)

## 5.11 RENT & PREMISES COSTS

### Lease Structure

**Common arrangements:**
1. **Fixed rent:** AUD $3,000–$8,000/month (varies by location, practice size)
2. **Percentage of turnover:** 5–8% of gross revenue (less common in VIC)
3. **Hybrid:** fixed base + percentage of excess

**Operator control points:**
- **At lease signing:** confirm lease term, renewal rights, break clauses, make-good obligations
- **Monthly:** rent invoice matches lease terms (value, payment date)
- **Annual:** review rent increases (usually CPI-linked)
- **Red flags:**
  - Rent paid but not invoiced (indicates informal arrangement = tax/legal risk)
  - Make-good costs at lease end not budgeted

### Landlord Outgoings

**Typically included in rent or charged separately:**
- Building insurance
- Council rates
- Common area maintenance
- Security/cleaning of common areas

**Operator control points:**
- **Monthly/quarterly:** outgoings invoice reconciled to lease
- **Red flags:**
  - Outgoings increasing unexpectedly (may indicate building maintenance)
  - No breakdown of outgoings (request itemised statement)

## 5.15 PATHOLOGY ARRANGEMENT

### Typical Model (Victorian Practice)

**Arrangement types:**
1. **Lease/Licence:** Practice provides space to pathology lab (e.g., Pathology Australia, collection centre); lab pays rent
2. **Service fee:** Pathology company pays practice commission per collection or per patient
3. **Revenue share:** Practice shares in test revenue (less common)
4. **No arrangement:** Collections done on-site; pathology company picks up and pays nothing

**Typical pathology companies:**
- Pathology Australia
- Australian Pathology
- Local diagnostic providers
- Hospital-based services

**Operator control points:**
- **At agreement:** confirm terms (rent paid to practice? commission structure? equipment provision?)
- **Monthly:** if revenue-share or commission, reconcile collections vs payment
- **Red flags:**
  - Collection kits not restocked (supplier responsibility)
  - Pathology company not remitting agreed rent/commission

## 5.19 WEEKLY OPERATOR CASH REVIEW CHECKLIST

**Every Monday morning (or start of week):**

1. **Bank balance:** check main account (should match last Friday close)
2. **Medicare deposits:** verify amount and count (should align with billings last week)
3. **EFTPOS deposits:** verify amounts and dates
4. **Payroll cleared:** confirm wages/super paid (should reflect timesheets)
5. **Rent paid:** confirm monthly rent cleared
6. **Outstanding invoices:** check any major suppliers not yet paid
7. **Cash register reconciliation:** (if using cash) daily float vs receipts
8. **Aged debtors:** identify invoices unpaid >30 days (private fees, gap fees)
9. **Pathology payments:** if applicable, confirm commission/rent received

**Red flags for investigation:**
- Missing deposits (claim not processed?)
- Delayed EFTPOS (fraud risk?)
- Payroll overdue (compliance issue)
- Large EFTPOS fees (renegotiate merchant terms?)
- Unpaid invoice from supplier (budget impact)

---

## 5.20 MONTHLY CASH RECONCILIATION

**End of each month:**

1. **Bank reconciliation:** all transactions matched to bank statement
2. **Clinic revenue summary:**
   - Total Medicare claims lodged
   - Total Medicare received (should lag by ~1 week)
   - Total private/gap fees invoiced
   - Total private/gap fees received
   - Total EFTPOS deposits
3. **Contractor GP payment:** calculate due amount and verify paid
4. **Payroll register:** total wages + super vs bank payments
5. **Supplier invoices:** all material invoices logged and categorised
6. **Loan/rent:** verify payments cleared
7. **Accounts receivable aging:** identify any debtors >60 days
8. **Profit & loss statement:** compare to budget (variance analysis)

**Documentation retained:** bank statements, reconciliation schedule, copies of major invoices

---

## 5.21 QUARTERLY CASH REVIEW

**Every quarter (end of Mar, Jun, Sep, Dec):**

1. **Tax obligation:** BAS/GST due? Confirm amount and lodgement
2. **Financial statement:** compare to budget (revenue, expenses, cash)
3. **Loan covenant compliance:** confirm with accountant (if required)
4. **Superannuation reconciliation:** verify all employer contributions made
5. **Payroll tax:** if over threshold, confirm quarterly return lodged
6. **Budget review:** adjust forecast if revenue/costs trending differently

# PART 7: STAFF, HR, PAYROLL & EMPLOYMENT CONTROL

## Overview

A GP practice's reputation, compliance, and financial performance depend entirely on staff. This section covers ALL employment control points from hiring through offboarding, with emphasis on Victorian/Australian legal obligations.

## 7.1 CONTRACTOR GP VS EMPLOYEE GP: THE SHAM CONTRACTING RISK

### Legal Framework

**Contractor classification (at risk for ATO challenge):**
- Independent contractor GP (works for themselves, provides service to clinic)
- Receives percentage of billings or flat fee
- No tax withheld by clinic
- No superannuation
- No leave entitlements

**Sham contracting red flags (ATO guidance & *Prince Developments Pty Ltd v K Hazel* 2021):**
- Clinic controls how, where, when services provided
- Clinic controls scheduling (roster system)
- Clinic dictates which patients GP sees
- Clinic supplies all equipment/software
- GP not free to refuse work
- GP cannot engage substitute
- High degree of supervision
- Clinic controls departure terms

**ATO approach (2021 onwards):**
- Increased scrutiny of medical practice contractor arrangements
- *Prince Developments* case established: doctor working clinic hours under clinic direction = likely employee (PAYG tax + super required)
- Non-compliance penalty: back tax + super + interest + 75% penalty

### Determining Status: Employee vs Contractor

**Contractor MUST have:**
1. Own ABN registered with ATO
2. Own tax file number (separate from clinic)
3. Own professional indemnity insurance (separate from clinic PII)
4. Written independent contractor agreement (RACGP template recommended)
5. Freedom to refuse work or request time off
6. Freedom to work elsewhere simultaneously
7. Own equipment/software (or clear lease arrangement)
8. Own business entity (sole trader or company)
9. Own superannuation fund
10. Invoice clinic for services (not placed on payroll)

**If ANY of above missing = likely employee classification (ATO perspective)**

### Employee GP MUST have:
1. Employment contract (not contractor agreement)
2. PAYG tax withheld (ATO-registered as employer)
3. Superannuation contributions (11.5%) to employee's fund
4. Leave entitlements (annual, personal, long service, parental)
5. Workers compensation insurance
6. Award compliance (if applicable – usually custom arrangements in medical)

### Operator Responsibility

**At hire:**
1. Decide: contractor or employee (with accountant advice)
2. If contractor:
   - Request ABN + ABN lookup confirmation (abn.business.gov.au)
   - Obtain signed RACGP contractor agreement
   - Request proof of PII (insurance certificate)
   - Confirm independent contractor declaration signed
   - NO superannuation contributions
   - NO tax withholding
3. If employee:
   - Obtain signed employment contract
   - Set up PAYG withholding in payroll (calculate based on tax scale)
   - Set up superannuation deduction (11.5%)
   - Ensure WorkCover insurance covers
   - Register with WorkSafe Victoria (if not already)

**Monthly (both types):**
- Verify contractor invoices vs billings (reconcile percentage or fee)
- Verify employee PAYG withheld correctly (check payslips)
- Verify superannuation contributions processed

## 7.2 NURSE EMPLOYMENT & CREDENTIALING

### Registration & Credentials

**Registered Nurse (RN):**
- Must be registered with AHPRA (Nursing and Midwifery Board of Australia)
- Check registration: **ahpra.gov.au > Find a Health Practitioner**
- Annual renewal required
- Scope of practice: administers medications, performs clinical procedures

**Enrolled Nurse (EN):**
- Also AHPRA-registered (different scope)
- Can assist RN, limited independent scope
- Check registration: AHPRA website

**Immunisation Nurse:**
- Often RN with vaccination certificate (separate from AHPRA)
- Must have current vaccination/immunisation certificate
- Check certificate expiry annually

### Award Coverage

**Applicable:** Nurses Award 2020 (Fair Work Ombudsman)

**Minimum rates (April 2026 — **external verification needed**):**
- RN Level 1 (entry): ~AUD $65,000–$70,000 pa
- RN Level 2: ~AUD $72,000–$78,000 pa
- EN Level 1: ~AUD $55,000–$60,000 pa

**Applies to:**
- Full-time nurses
- Part-time nurses (pro-rated)
- Casual nurses (slightly higher hourly rate to compensate for no leave)

**Penalty rates:**
- Evening shift: ~15% loading
- Weekend: ~50% loading (Saturday) / ~100% (Sunday)
- Public holiday: ~150–250% loading

**Operator control points:**
- **At hire:** obtain AHPRA certificate number; create reminder for annual check
- **Payroll:** ensure Award rate applied (benchmark quarterly against Fair Work)
- **Timesheets:** record all hours; flag unrecorded/unpaid work
- **Superannuation:** 11.5% contributions mandatory

### Credentialing File (Nurse)

**Must contain:**
- AHPRA certificate (photocopy; verified against website)
- Employment contract (signed)
- References (2–3, from previous employers)
- Police check (if required by clinic policy)
- Immunisation record (if administering vaccines)
  - Hepatitis B immunity
  - MMR immunity
  - COVID-19 vaccination status
  - Influenza vaccination (annual)
- Vaccination certificate (if immunisation nurse)
- Training records
  - CPR (annual, must be current)
  - Infection control training (annual)
  - Privacy & confidentiality (annual)
  - PRODA/My Health Record (if applicable)
  - Bloodborne pathogens / sharps safety
- Working With Children Check (if applicable – depends on role, varies by state)
- Signed code of conduct / confidentiality agreement
- Performance review records (annual)

**Retention:** 7 years after employment ends (Fair Work Act minimum)

## 7.6 LEAVE MANAGEMENT

### Annual Leave

**Entitlement (Award/National Employment Standards):**
- Full-time: 4 weeks per year
- Part-time: pro-rata (based on hours worked)
- Accrues month-by-month or weekly (depends on agreement)

**Loading:** 17.5% penalty loading if not taken (or paid out at termination)

**Operator control:**
- Maintain leave register (date, duration, balance)
- Approve leave requests 2–4 weeks in advance
- Ensure minimum staffing maintained during leave periods
- Pay out untaken leave on termination (rarely withheld)

### Personal Leave (Sick & Carer's Leave)

**Entitlement:**
- Full-time: 10 days per year (can be used for own illness or caring for family)
- Part-time: pro-rata
- Casual: no entitlement (can request unpaid leave)

**Operator control:**
- Maintain separate leave register
- Request medical certificate if >2 consecutive days absent (or as per policy)
- Cannot refuse reasonable personal leave request
- Unused personal leave does NOT accrue or pay out (expires each year in most cases)

### Long Service Leave

**Entitlement:**
- Accrues after 10 years of service (1.3 weeks per year, capped at 13 weeks for ongoing employees)
- Can be taken as week-long breaks or saved
- Rarely used in small GP practices (high staff turnover)

**Operator control:**
- Maintain record of accrual
- Discuss options near 10-year mark
- Pay out on termination after 10 years of service

### Parental Leave

**Entitlement:**
- 12 months unpaid (per National Employment Standards)
- Some employers offer paid (discretionary)

**Operator responsibility:**
- Confirm in writing within 4 weeks of notification
- Discuss return-to-work arrangements
- Hold role open (or equivalent) for 12 months

## 7.7 ROSTERING & TIMESHEETS

### Rostering System

**Good practice:**
- Roster planned 4–6 weeks in advance
- Published on staff notice board + online system (if available)
- Staff can request days off (reasonable notice required)
- Clinic can refuse if impact on operations too high
- Changes to roster: minimum 2 weeks notice (unless emergency)

**Operator control points:**
- **Monthly:** review roster against staffing levels (no understaffing)
- **Avoid:** regular overtime (indicates understaffing); if used, ensure paid fairly
- **Fairness:** rotate unpopular shifts (evenings, weekends) fairly across team

### Timesheets

**For all staff (except GPs, who invoice separately):**

**Process:**
- Staff record daily hours (start time, finish time, breaks)
- Fortnightly/weekly signature/confirmation by practice manager
- Cross-check against roster (flag discrepancies)
- Feed into payroll system
- Retain 5+ years

**Operator control:**
- **Daily:** monitor timesheets for obvious errors (e.g., 12-hour shifts without overtime recorded)
- **Weekly:** sign off timesheets; ensure total hours match contracted hours
- **Monthly:** total hours payroll vs hours paid (variance investigation)
- **Red flags:**
  - Staff working unpaid hours (indicate systemic understaffing or informal arrangement)
  - Timesheet "ghosting" (staff forget to record, estimate at end of week)

## 7.8 CONTRACTOR GP PAYMENT PROCESS

**Weekly/fortnightly process:**

1. **Billings report:** run from Best Practice (or practice management system)
   - Filter by contractor GP name
   - Sum all items billed (Medicare + private) for the period
   
2. **Calculate payment:**
   - Billings × percentage (e.g., 70%) = amount due
   - Or: flat fee + (excess billings × percentage)
   
3. **Cross-check:**
   - Compare this week's billings to previous weeks (unusual spike = investigate)
   - Ensure items claimed to Medicare are included (cross-ref Medicare receipt list)
   - Verify no duplicate entries
   
4. **Generate invoice/payment schedule:**
   - Create payment slip showing calculation (GPs appreciate transparency)
   - Schedule payment to GP's bank account
   
5. **Record in general ledger:**
   - GP contractor expense account
   - Match payment to invoice (audit trail)

**Red flags:**
- GP payment > 75% (losing too much to contractor)
- GP payment < 60% (GP may claim underpayment; verify contract)
- Sudden drop in GP billings (investigate: patient transfers? items coded incorrectly?)
- GP disputes calculation (maintain clear records to defend)

## 7.9 PAYROLL APPROVAL & CONTROLS

### Payroll Process (Weekly or Fortnightly)

1. **Timesheet approval:** practice manager signs off all timesheets
2. **Payroll run:** bookkeeper/payroll software calculates gross pay, PAYG, super, deductions
3. **Payroll report:** bookkeeper provides summary (who's paid, how much, deductions)
4. **Owner approval:** owner reviews and approves before payment
5. **Payment:** salary processed to staff bank accounts
6. **Documentation:** payslips issued to staff; retained for 7 years

### PAYG Withholding Accuracy

**Common errors:**
- Wrong tax scale (check ATO update quarterly)
- Incorrect salary basis (e.g., forget to include super in gross pay)
- Tax-free threshold not claimed (if applicable)
- Multiple job withholding not adjusted

**Operator check:**
- **Monthly:** spot-check 2–3 payslips (gross, tax withheld, net)
- **Quarterly:** compare total tax withheld to BAS; should be close match
- **Annual:** staff can claim refund if over-withheld, so small surpluses acceptable

### Superannuation Processing

**Mandatory (11.5% from 1 July 2025):**
- Calculate 11.5% of gross salary
- Deposit to employee's SuperFund (employee nominates)
- Within 28 days of end of pay cycle (or monthly)

**Operator check:**
- **Monthly:** verify super deposited to employee funds
- **Quarterly:** BAS/reconciliation shows total super paid
- **Annual:** obtain donation receipts from SuperFund (matches total paid)

**Common issues:**
- Super paid late (employees can complain to ATO; clinic faces penalties)
- Wrong amount (under-contribution)
- Paid to wrong fund (employee must verify)

## 7.11 PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT & DISCIPLINE

### Informal Process (for minor issues)

1. **Conversation:** manager addresses issue privately, same day if possible
2. **Documentation:** manager notes conversation (date, issue, outcome)
3. **Improvement:** agree on improvement and follow-up date
4. **Re-check:** manager confirms improvement at follow-up
5. **Record:** file in staff member's performance file

**Examples:** missed timelines, minor errors, attitude issue

### Formal Performance Management (for serious issues)

**Step 1: Informal warning (if first offence)**
- Verbal discussion documented in writing
- Copy provided to staff member
- 2-week improvement period
- Re-check and document outcome

**Step 2: Formal warning (if issue persists or serious breach)**
- Written warning letter
- State exact issue, expected standard, consequences of further breach
- Right to respond in writing
- Improvement plan (2–4 weeks)
- Re-check and document

**Step 3: Disciplinary action or termination**
- Final written warning or dismissal (depending on severity)
- Follow Fair Work requirements (no harsh, unjust termination)
- Provide all documentation to staff member
- Reasonable opportunity to respond

**Operator responsibility:**
- Document EVERY performance issue in writing (date, issue, outcome)
- Act quickly but fairly
- Don't gossip about performance issues (confidentiality)
- If dismissal is likely, consult legal counsel

## 7.12 TERMINATION & OFFBOARDING

### Notice Periods

**For employees:**
- Minimum notice (Fair Work Act): 1–4 weeks depending on tenure
- Most employment contracts specify longer (2–4 weeks common)
- Immediate termination only for serious misconduct (consult legal counsel first)

**For contractors:**
- Per contractor agreement (typically 2–4 weeks)
- Check contract before terminating

**Operator action:**
- If terminating: provide written notice (minimum 2 weeks is standard)
- State last day of work and final pay date
- Arrange handover
- Plan for backfill

### Final Pay

**What's included:**
- Salary to last day of work
- Accrued annual leave (paid out in full)
- Personal leave (not paid out unless agreement allows)
- Long service leave (paid out if >10 years service)
- Superannuation (any final contributions)

**What's deducted:**
- Any equipment not returned (if agreed in contract)
- Any overpayment (if agreed with employee)
- Debt to clinic (rare and contentious; legal advice needed)

**Operator check:**
- **Within 2 weeks:** issue final payslip
- **Before last day:** collect all keys, access cards, equipment
- **On last day:** obtain signed equipment return form
- **After departure:** remove system access (email, PRODA, Best Practice, etc.)

### Offboarding Checklist

**Before departure:**
- [ ] Handover meeting (identify in-progress work, patient handover, etc.)
- [ ] Training for replacement begun
- [ ] Patient files organised (ensure continuity)
- [ ] GP/nurse: identify which patients transfer to which practitioner

**At departure:**
- [ ] All equipment returned (stethoscope, laptop, ID badge, etc.)
- [ ] Keys & access cards surrendered
- [ ] System access disabled (email, PRODA, Best Practice, My Health Record)
- [ ] Exit interview conducted (feedback on role, workplace)
- [ ] Final payslip issued
- [ ] References provided (if requested, at clinic discretion)

**After departure:**
- [ ] Credential file archived
- [ ] Employment record retained (7 years minimum)
- [ ] AHPRA registration no longer relied upon (patients transferred)
- [ ] Tax & superannuation finalised with ATO

## 7.13 INCIDENT REPORTING & INVESTIGATION

### Duty to Report

**Incidents requiring documentation:**
- Patient safety incident (near-miss, harm)
- Staff injury or near-miss (WorkSafe obligation)
- Infection control breach (e.g., needle stick, spill)
- Data security breach (patient data exposure)
- Medication error
- Staff conflict or complaint
- Equipment failure

**Process:**
1. **Incident report form:** completed by person present (or manager if not available)
2. **Date, time, location, people involved:** key details
3. **Description:** what happened, what was noticed
4. **Immediate action taken:** (first aid, isolation, reporting to supervisor)
5. **Investigation:** manager interviews involved parties, reviews records
6. **Finding:** what was the root cause?
7. **Corrective action:** what will be done to prevent recurrence?
8. **Documentation:** report filed, reviewed by owner/manager

**Retention:** 7 years minimum (relevant to WorkSafe, Privacy Commissioner investigations)

### WorkSafe Victoria Reporting Obligation

**Must notify WorkSafe if:**
- Staff member injured (even if minor) during work
- Serious injury (serious bodily injury, serious illness, death)
- Dangerous occurrence (collapse of structure, exposure to chemical, etc.)

**Timeline:**
- Serious injury: notify WorkSafe ASAP (phone 1300 366 779, then written notification)
- Non-serious injury: notify within 10 days (written form)

**Operator responsibility:**
- Know which injuries require notification
- Report promptly (late reporting incurs penalty)
- Cooperate with WorkSafe investigation
- Implement recommendations from investigation

## 7.14 BULLYING, HARASSMENT & COMPLAINTS

### Fair Work Act Obligations

**Clinic must prevent:**
- Bullying (repeated, unreasonable conduct creating risk to health/safety)
- Harassment (conduct based on protected attribute: age, sex, disability, etc.)
- Discrimination (unfair treatment based on attribute)
- Sexual harassment

**Operator responsibility:**
- Have written anti-bullying, anti-harassment policy
- Make policy known to all staff
- Train staff on what constitutes bullying/harassment
- Investigate complaints seriously & impartially
- Support complainant (no victimisation)
- Disciplinary action against perpetrator (if substantiated)

### Complaint Handling Process

1. **Informal complaint:** staff member raises issue with manager verbally
   - Manager listens, documents (date, issue, person's account)
   - Manager investigates (interview other parties, review records)
   - Manager resolves (if possible) and documents outcome
   
2. **Formal complaint:** if informal not resolved or serious issue
   - Written complaint lodged with owner/practice manager
   - Owner acknowledges within 5 business days
   - Owner investigates (separate from manager, if manager involved)
   - Owner interviews all parties
   - Owner provides written response within 10 business days (minimum)
   - Outcome documented, filed

3. **Escalation:** if not resolved, staff member can lodge with Fair Work Commission (FWC)
   - Clinic has 10 business days to respond
   - FWC conciliation or arbitration

**Red flags:**
- Complaint not documented (defensible position lost)
- Delayed response (indicates lack of seriousness)
- Complaining party moved/demoted (victimisation claim)

## 7.15 WORKERS COMPENSATION & WORKPLACE INJURY

### WorkCover Victoria Insurance

**Mandatory if:** clinic has employees (even 1 part-time employee)

**Coverage:**
- Medical treatment (doctor, hospital, surgery, therapy)
- Wages replacement (60% weekly for up to 26 weeks; then can review)
- Rehabilitation
- Return-to-work assistance

**Premium calculation:**
- Based on payroll + risk class (medical practice = low-moderate risk, ~0.5–1.5%)
- Annual review & adjustment if payroll changes

**Operator responsibility:**
- Maintain current certificate of currency
- Notify insurer of new staff, payroll changes
- Report injuries to insurer within 10 days
- Cooperate with rehabilitation provider
- Keep injured worker informed

### Injury Notification & Documentation

**If staff member injured at work:**

1. **Immediate:** first aid/medical treatment
2. **Report to manager:** incident report form completed
3. **Notify WorkCover:** within 10 days (written form or online)
4. **Cooperate with insurer:** they manage claim, treatment, wages
5. **Rehabilitation:** comply with return-to-work plan
6. **Retainage:** file incident report, worker statement, doctor's reports (7 years minimum)

# PART 8: REGISTRATIONS, IDENTIFIERS, ACCESS & COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST

## Overview

A GP practice operates across multiple government, regulatory, and private systems. Each requires separate registration, identifiers, and access management. This section is a complete checklist of what exists, what doesn't, what needs to be verified, and what goes wrong if neglected.

---

## 8.1 CLINIC/BUSINESS-LEVEL REGISTRATIONS

### ABN & ACN

**What it is:**
- ABN (Australian Business Number): 11-digit unique identifier for all business activities
- ACN (Australian Company Number): if structure is Pty Ltd (company)

**Why it matters:**
- Required for Medicare claiming
- Required for tax (PAYG, GST)
- Required for business bank account
- Required for PayPal, merchant services, etc.

**Who controls access:**
- ABN: registrations open (public); no password needed
- ACN: ASIC register (public); structure details (shareholding) may be private or public

**Evidence it exists:**
- ABN lookup: abn.business.gov.au (search by name or ABN number)
- ACN lookup: asic.gov.au > Company Search (if company)
- ABN notification letter (from ATO when business registered)

**Renewal/maintenance:**
- ABN: permanent (no renewal, but must notify ATO if name/address changes)
- ACN: annual fees to ASIC; company must lodge financial statements annually

**Confirmed from docs / needs verification:**
- [ ] ABN lookup online confirms correct business name, address, structure
- [ ] If company: ACN lookup confirms director names, shareholding, status (active)

---

### Business Name & Structure

**What it is:**
- Business trading name (may differ from ABN name)
- Structure: sole trader, partnership, trust, company, or franchise

**Why it matters:**
- Determines tax treatment
- Determines liability (sole trader = personal liability; company = limited)
- Affects superannuation obligations
- Affects buy-sell/succession arrangements

**Evidence it exists:**
- ABN notification (shows structure)
- Trust deed (if trust structure)
- Partnership agreement (if partnership)
- ASIC company details (if Pty Ltd)

**Confirmed from docs / needs verification:**
- [ ] ABN name matches trading name (or authorised alias registered)
- [ ] Structure confirmed (sole trader? trust? company?)
- [ ] If trust: trust deed obtained and reviewed by accountant
- [ ] If partnership: partnership agreement exists and reviewed by legal counsel

---

### Medicare Location Registration

**What it is:**
- Practice is registered with Medicare as a healthcare provider location
- Required to claim Medicare benefits
- Separate from individual provider registration

**Why it matters:**
- Without location registration, clinic cannot lodge Medicare claims
- Affects practice incentives, grants, and funding eligibility
- Must match to PRODA & HPI-O

**Who controls access:**
- Medicare (Department of Health)
- Delegate: practice manager, bookkeeper, or designated staff member

**Evidence it exists:**
- Medicare location identifier (8-digit code, visible in Best Practice / MD system)
- Medicare registration letter (from Department of Health, may be archived)

**Renewal / maintenance:**
- Once registered, no formal renewal
- But must notify Medicare if details change (location address, GPs on site, etc.)

**What goes wrong if neglected:**
- Claims rejected (claims software won't process without location ID)
- Practice cash flow interrupted (no Medicare deposits)
- Accreditation affected (auditors require proof of Medicare location status)

**Confirmed from docs / needs verification:**
- [ ] Medicare location ID exists and matches to clinic address
- [ ] Location ID entered correctly in practice management software
- [ ] Test claim submitted to verify acceptance by Medicare

---

### Practice Incentives Program (PIP) Registration

**What it is:**
- Australian government program providing financial incentives for meeting quality/service targets
- Administered by Services Australia
- Practice must be registered to receive payments

**Why it matters:**
- PIP payments: typically AUD $10,000–$50,000+ per year depending on targets achieved
- Missing PIP registration = lost income
- Targets cover: patient safety, chronic disease management, mental health, Indigenous health, etc.

**Evidence it exists:**
- PIP registration letter (from Services Australia)
- PIP portal access (Services Australia online)
- Quarterly PIP payments visible in bank statement/practice management system

**Renewal / maintenance:**
- Annual renewal (must re-confirm eligibility)
- Quarterly reporting of performance metrics (may be automatic if PRODA linked)

**What goes wrong if neglected:**
- PIP payments delayed or lost
- Quarterly reporting missed = payment suspension
- Targets not met = no payment for that quarter

**Confirmed from docs / needs verification:**
- [ ] PIP registration letter obtained
- [ ] PIP portal access tested (login successful, current practice details visible)
- [ ] Last 4 quarters of PIP payments visible in bank account
- [ ] Quarterly performance targets reviewed & understood

---

### Workforce Incentive Program (WIP)

**What it is:**
- Government funding for rural/remote practices or practices meeting specific workforce goals
- Administered by Services Australia
- Provides grants for hiring nurses, allied health, etc.

**Why it matters:**
- If clinic in eligible area: potential AUD $50,000–$200,000+ in annual funding
- Attached to specific staffing requirements (must hire RN, psychologist, etc.)
- Miss requirements = repayment of grant

**Eligibility:**
- Practice in rural or remote area (by postcode), OR
- Meets specific service provision goals

**Evidence it exists:**
- WIP grant agreement (Services Australia)
- WIP portal access

**Renewal / maintenance:**
- Annual; must confirm ongoing eligibility & staffing

**What goes wrong if neglected:**
- Grant payments lost
- If already received: may have to repay if staffing requirement not maintained
- Clinic loses competitive advantage in recruitment (WIP funding often used to attract staff)

**Confirmed from docs / needs verification:**
- [ ] Determine if clinic eligible (by location or service model)
- [ ] If eligible: WIP registration obtained; payments received
- [ ] WIP staffing requirements understood & documented
- [ ] Compliance with staffing requirements confirmed

---

### HPI-O (Healthcare Provider Identifier – Organisation)

**What it is:**
- 16-character code issued by Services Australia (HPOS system)
- Unique identifier for the practice as a healthcare provider
- Required for integration with My Health Record, PBS, e-referrals, etc.

**Why it matters:**
- Enables secure electronic communication with hospitals, specialists, pathologists
- Required for PBS (Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme) access
- Required for eReferral systems
- Identity verification for secure messaging (NASH)

**Evidence it exists:**
- HPI-O code issued by Services Australia / HPOS
- Letter or email confirming HPI-O
- May be visible in Best Practice or practice management software

**Renewal / maintenance:**
- Assigned once; no renewal
- Must update if practice details change (address, ownership, etc.)

**What goes wrong if neglected:**
- Cannot integrate with My Health Record (eHealth services broken)
- Cannot access PBS online
- eReferral systems may not recognize practice

**Confirmed from docs / needs verification:**
- [ ] HPI-O code obtained from HPOS
- [ ] HPI-O code matches to practice name & address
- [ ] HPI-O loaded into Best Practice/practice management system
- [ ] Test integration with My Health Record successful

---

### NASH Certificate (National Authentication Service for Health)

**What it is:**
- Security credential (digital certificate) issued by Services Australia
- Enables secure communication with My Health Record, PBS, eReferral, etc.
- Installed on clinic server or accessed via web portal

**Why it matters:**
- Essential for secure clinical messaging
- Enables two-factor authentication to government health systems
- Protects patient data in transit

**Evidence it exists:**
- NASH certificate details (certificate number, expiry date)
- Visible in Best Practice or medical software (if integrated)
- HPOS system shows active NASH certificate

**Renewal / maintenance:**
- Expires typically every 2–3 years; must renew before expiry
- Renewal done via HPOS or Services Australia

**What goes wrong if neglected:**
- Certificate expires = lose secure access to My Health Record, PBS, eReferral
- Clinical workflow interrupted
- Electronic prescribing blocked

**Confirmed from docs / needs verification:**
- [ ] NASH certificate obtained & installed
- [ ] Expiry date noted; calendar reminder set for renewal (3 months before expiry)
- [ ] Certificate tested (login to HPOS successful)

---

### My Health Record Participation & Policy

**What it is:**
- Practice enrolled in My Health Record (patient health records stored in national system)
- Practice commits to contributing patient data to My Health Record
- Requires My Health Record participation policy

**Why it matters:**
- Enables care coordination across practitioners & hospitals
- Improves patient safety (medication reconciliation, allergy alerts)
- Required for accreditation (AGPAL, QPA)
- Required for some government funding (PIP, WIP)

**Evidence it exists:**
- My Health Record registration confirmation (from Services Australia / HPOS)
- My Health Record participation policy (written document)
- Practice can access My Health Record portal (test access)

**Policy must cover:**
- How practice accesses & uses patient records
- Confidentiality & security measures
- Patient privacy rights
- Breach notification process
- Data retention & deletion

**Renewal / maintenance:**
- Registration is ongoing (no formal renewal)
- Policy review: annual minimum
- Compliance audit: as per accreditation cycle

**What goes wrong if neglected:**
- Practice cannot participate in My Health Record (integration broken)
- Accreditation at risk
- Funding (PIP/WIP) at risk
- Patient safety concerns

**Confirmed from docs / needs verification:**
- [ ] My Health Record registration confirmation obtained
- [ ] Participation policy exists (written, communicated to staff)
- [ ] Test access to My Health Record portal successful (can view patient records)
- [ ] Staff training on My Health Record completed

---

### PRODA Organisation Registration

**What it is:**
- Practice Registration and Disclosures Authority registration
- Enables practice staff to use government health systems (HPOS, NASH, Medicare, PBS, eReferral, etc.)
- Practice manager typically holds organisation admin account

**Why it matters:**
- Without PRODA registration, staff cannot submit Medicare claims, access HPOS, or use PBS
- PRODA tracks who has access to which systems (audit trail)
- Security-critical (must restrict access to authorized staff only)

**Evidence it exists:**
- PRODA organisation account active (can log in)
- Delegations list showing authorized staff members
- Delegation notification letters (one per staff member)

**Delegations:**
- Practice manager: typically has admin rights (can add/remove other users)
- Bookkeeper: can submit Medicare claims, access bank reconciliation
- GP: can access HPOS, prescriber details, PBS
- Reception: can view/update patient details, submit claims
- Others: as appropriate to role

**Renewal / maintenance:**
- PRODA account active as long as practice is registered
- Annual review of delegations (remove departed staff, add new staff)
- If staff member leaves: immediately revoke access in PRODA

**What goes wrong if neglected:**
- Staff member leaves but retains PRODA access (security risk; could fraudulently claim Medicare)
- New staff cannot submit claims (waiting time for access = cash flow delays)
- Auditors cannot verify who submitted claims (compliance failure)
- Departed staff submit fraudulent claims (practice liable)

**Confirmed from docs / needs verification:**
- [ ] PRODA organisation account exists & active (test login)
- [ ] Current delegations list obtained (who has what access?)
- [ ] Cross-check delegations against current staff list (remove inactive users)
- [ ] Test staff member access (e.g., reception logs in successfully)
- [ ] Document change log: when were delegations last reviewed/updated?

---
